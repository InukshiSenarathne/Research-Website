I-NAC, Smart Recruiting System streamlines recruitment processes by addressing individual job requirements and aligning candidates with company goals. It consists of four stages: Resume Parsing, NER Tagging, Big Data Analysis, Chatbot communication, and a Human Resource Management Portal. The system uses advanced NLP models, Big 5 models, and deep-learning to improve objectivity and efficiency. The system also integrates behavior analysis and candidate selection data, promoting cross-training and targeted development.

LITERATURE SURVEY

This project aims to automate resume screening and information extraction using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, using popular libraries like NLTK and Spacy. The project focuses on enhancing efficiency and reducing bias in the hiring process. Big data offers comprehensive information, improving HR management and fostering industry growth. The paper v v system introduces a paper v v system that enables efficient data processing, diverse data sources, real-time analysis, and effective data management. The JARO chatbot automates candidate interviews using AI, incorporating technologies like Google Dialogflow and Firebase. The study investigates the role of employee status in the relationship between perceived organizational support and emotional organizational commitment, with low-status employees showing higher improvements. The study also highlights the importance of user experience in effective human-computer interaction on mobile devices, demonstrating that even novice users can complete tasks effectively with a useful tool, and participants of all experience levels perform better in real-world settings than in lab settings.

METHODOLOGY

Advanced machine learning (NLP) models have been used to extract essential details from resumes, including applicant skills, work experience, and academic achievements. One common NLP text reading model is the Named Entity Recognition (NER) model. Three methods are used to extract text from CV PDF files: pyMuPDF, which handles different PDF versions and extracts text even from scanned PDFs; Entity Ruler, which allows for custom entity recognition based on Sri Lankan entities; and Word2Vec, which measures semantic similarity between extracted CV data and predefined templates or keywords specific to the Sri Lankan job market. These methods ensure accurate recognition and extraction of relevant entities, improving the productivity and relevance of the output.

Big data in human resource management enhances procedures such as hiring, development, training, performance, pay, and overall corporate performance. HR departments are responsible for finding top talent for companies by selecting qualified individuals for interviews. The Big Data Analyzing Process aims to analyze extracted data from CVs to select the best candidates for recruitment. The process consists of three components: predicting the skill set for a job role, selecting suitable candidates, and filtering candidates through a scoring method. Data is collected through a database that extracts data from received CVs and categorizes them according to recognized categories. The system predicts the required skill set for a job role, and the top-scored candidates are sent to the next step in the hiring process.

The research aims to develop an automated interview system for IT recruitment, utilizing data collection, system design, and performance evaluation. The system uses audio, video, and text transcripts to assess job-specific knowledge and communication skills. The system's accuracy, reliability, and efficiency are assessed through experiments using advanced techniques like acoustic modeling, voice ontology, Word2Vec, TFIDF model, and neural network models. This methodology enhances the recruitment process by reducing interviewer bias, standardizing evaluations, and improving efficiency.

The system will also analyze customers past traffic violation record history and classify customers (Claimant) as risky, safe and medium driver based on it to predict the premium plan for the following year. Statistical and mathematical model will be used to create a personalized premium calculation model which includes all the features required by the insurance company for the model. The system should also ensure the fairness in these calculations.

The system incorporates behavior analysis and candidate selection into a model that detects high-level scenarios based on job requirements. Predefined KPIs visually present the analyzed data. The data is processed and analyzed using a pipeline for extraction, transformation, and loading. The system can choose attributes like performance evaluations, time spent on learning activities, and training completion rates. Decision trees and random forests are used to increase prediction accuracy. The model is trained and evaluated for performance improvement.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The goal of this research problem is to find a way to automate the process of recruiting potential candidates in a traditional setting, potentially replacing the traditional human-to-human approach with a computer-based system. This would allow employers to identify the best candidate for a specific job opening in a much more efficient manner [6]. The current research in this area is focused on finding ways to make the process more accurate and reliable. This involves a combination of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) techniques, which allow the computer system to analyze a candidate's responses to the questions asked through an AI Chatbot and determine whether the candidate is a good fit for the job [4]. Additionally, the system can also be used to identify any potential biases or inappropriate questions during the recruitment process and alert the employer, allowing for more equitable decision-making [2] [3] [5]. Ultimately, the goal of this research is to provide a more efficient and fair way of recruiting candidates, allowing employers to select the best-fit candidate for any given job [1]. This could lead to improved job satisfaction and productivity for employers, as well as this will ensure that the right candidates are identified and hired more quickly and efficiently, resulting in a more effective workforce
